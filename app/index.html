The gap

Today’s playbook is reactive: mempool racing and pause switches engage after damage is in motion. Moat targets the same high-risk calls—before state commits.

Why now

  • Shipping is faster; audit coverage does not keep pace with every deploy.
  • Hypernative Firewall is closed, proprietary, enterprise-priced—out of reach for many teams.
  • OpenZeppelin Defender is sunsetting (July 2026), leaving an ecosystem hole.

What Moat is

An open-source, decentralized layer any team can integrate: emulate → rule-check → attest → execute or timelock. Auditable building blocks, not a black box.

Built for protocol teams

Shrink the attack surface your auditors never had time to re-read.

LPs and partners

Point to concrete gates: which functions are protected, what “normal” means in your system, and how users keep recourse if something is wrong.

Your invariants

Value limits, balance ratios, yield anomalies, frequency caps—rules written by the team that understands the protocol, not a generic risk score.

Composable floor

Cover the critical path first; layer Forta, Hypernative, or custom engines as you mature. Moat is the baseline, not the ceiling.

How it works

Submit → validate → attest → execute—or wait the timelock.

  1. 1
    On-chain gate A modifier on critical functions (Forta Firewall–style proxy pattern)—open source and battle-tested in production.
  2. 2
    Off-chain emulation Operators simulate before/after state, run your rule set, and co-sign attestations—targeting Symbiotic / EigenLayer AVS patterns.
  3. 3
    User fallback If a tx is held, a configurable window (e.g. 30 minutes) gives your team time to respond; then the user can proceed. Always.

Stack
